# Theoretical Foundations: Location, Location, Location

The "Location, Location, Location" chapter rests on a rich foundation of cross-disciplinary theory. What follows is not an academic exercise — it is evidence that the coordinates are not invented. They are discovered. The same structures appear across physics, biology, economics, mathematics, sociology, psychology, and philosophy. The framework simply names them for the purpose of conflict.

---

## The Johari Window Connection

The six coordinates are a specialized, high-stakes adaptation of the Johari Window (Luft & Ingham, 1955). The mapping is direct:

| Johari Window | EBOLA Coordinate |
|---------------|------------------|
| **Blind Spot** (known to others, hidden from self) | BLIND SPOT |
| **Hidden Area / Façade** (known to self, hidden from others) | GAME |
| **Unknown** (known to neither) | HORIZON |
| **Open Area** (known to all) | POSITION, WANT |

The difference is purpose. Johari was designed for team communication and trust-building. The EBOLA coordinates are designed for conflict where the stakes are high and sight is obstructed. 

The framework also adds what Johari lacks: THE MACHINE — the recognition that when multiple people's Blind Spots, Games, and Horizons click together, they produce coordinated outcomes nobody specifically intended. Thirteen witnesses, one biased judge, one hidden architect, one manufactured verdict. No conspiracy. Just coordinates aligning into a system that builds itself.

## The Physics Connection

The coordinates rest on two physical laws. 

**The Observer Effect:** You cannot measure a system without changing it. There is no neutral observation — presence is participation. The "Observer" category exists in the framework only to prove it almost never applies.

**Relativity:** Truth looks different depending on your frame of reference. There is no center of the conflict, only different velocities relative to it. POSITION is your frame. What looks like "the truth" from your frame may be unrecognizable from theirs. 

The framework doesn't ask "what is true?" — it asks "true from where?"

## The Biology Connection

Organisms don't just live in environments — they build them. Biologists call this **Niche Construction**. A beaver builds a dam. A father collects 13 witness statements. Both are reshaping the ecosystem to ensure survival. 

WANT and FEAR drive this: all biological entities act to maintain **homeostasis**. When threatened, we secrete narratives like a squid secretes ink — not from malice, but from the survival instinct. 

The framework treats conflict as niche competition: parties altering the environment to make it hospitable to themselves and hostile to others. The Machine is simply multiple organisms constructing the same niche simultaneously, often without coordination.

## The Economics Connection

The coordinates map directly onto **Game Theory** and **Information Asymmetry**. 

GAME is strategic information — what you know that you hide to maximize your utility. The New Partner plays a zero-sum game: for her to win, the mother must lose. She hides her involvement to avoid transaction costs (legal penalties, social shame). 

WANT is utility function. FEAR is loss aversion. 

The Machine is not conspiracy — it's a set of misaligned incentives producing predictable, suboptimal outcomes. People don't do what is right; they do what they are incentivized to do. The framework makes incentives visible so they can be examined, not just obeyed.

## The Mathematics Connection

HORIZON is a topological limit. In any complex system, there are truths that cannot be proven from within that system — this is **Gödel's Incompleteness**. 

The Child cannot see the Machine because the Child is a subset of it. You cannot fully map a room while locked in a box in its corner. 

BLIND SPOT isn't stupidity; it's a logical necessity of being a point on a graph rather than the person holding the pen. The framework acknowledges this: you will never see everything. But you can mark where your vision ends — and that marking is itself a form of sight.

## The Standpoint Theory Connection

**Standpoint Theory** (rooted in feminist sociology and Marxist thought) argues that knowledge is situated — your structural position determines what you are capable of knowing. 

HORIZON is pure standpoint epistemology. The Journalist using passive voice for Palestinian deaths isn't lying; her structural position (the house style, the newsroom, the template) makes the grammar of erasure invisible to her. She genuinely cannot see it. 

The framework doesn't accuse people of deception where structural blindness is the cause. It separates BLIND SPOT (active self-deception) from HORIZON (positional impossibility) precisely because Standpoint Theory demands the distinction.

---

## The Systems Theory Connection

**Second-Order Cybernetics** argues that the observer is not outside the system — the observer is part of the system being observed. 

Traditional conflict resolution treats the mediator as neutral, watching a machine from above. The framework argues the opposite: there is no above. The 13 witnesses, the new partner, the judge — each believes they are a separate component. They are actually a single closed-loop system producing a predetermined verdict. 

"An Observer watches the weather. A Party builds the storm." 

The moment you watch, you're in the loop. The framework makes loop membership visible so you can see the machine you're part of, not just the parts you think you're watching.

---

## The Cognitive Bias Connection

WANT, FEAR, and BLIND SPOT are grounded in **Motivated Reasoning** — the psychological finding that we process information to reach conclusions we prefer. 

We don't just "miss" facts; we actively (though often unconsciously) filter them to avoid loss (FEAR) and achieve desired outcomes (WANT). 

The Two Archives scenario illustrates this: people don't choose testimony based on evidence quality. They reach for the archive that supports the bricks they're already laying. 

The framework externalizes this filtering so you can see your own motivated reasoning in action — not to eliminate bias (impossible) but to account for it.

---

## The Perspectivism Connection (Nietzsche)

Nietzsche argued there is no "God's eye view" — truth is not a single objective landscape but a collection of interpretations from different angles. The more perspectives you gather, the richer your concept. 

The Coordinate Card is a tool for radical **perspectivism**: it forces the user to admit their truth is a view from somewhere. 

The Meta Question ("Guess Their Coordinates") extends this — if you cannot describe their WANT, FEAR, GAME, and BLIND SPOT as precisely as your own, you haven't seen them. You've only seen your story about them. 

Truth isn't found in one archive. It's in the friction between them.

---

## The Phenomenology Connection (Heidegger, Merleau-Ponty)

**Phenomenology** studies the structures of experience. Its central insight: we are "thrown" into a world that already has meaning. 

HORIZON is a phenomenological term — the limit of what can be experienced from a situated body. You don't just "see" a conflict; you experience it through the weight of your history, your role, your physical proximity to the storm. 

The Two Children are thrown into a structure they didn't build. Their grief, their sense of whose death "counts," their entire reality — constructed by the horizon they inherited. 

POSITION (Inherited) names this: you didn't choose the structure, but you're in it.

---

## The Existentialist Connection (Sartre)

Sartre insisted we are always choosing, even when we pretend we aren't. To claim neutrality is **Bad Faith** (mauvaise foi). 

The framework's Reality Check — "if you are reading this to win, you are a Party" — is a direct challenge to Bad Faith. By naming GAME and WANT, the framework moves the user from passive victim of the Machine to active Agent responsible for the bricks they carry. 

The Judge who prays he won't "slip up" is in Bad Faith: pretending the role of "Judge" exempts the human underneath from having already decided. 

The framework refuses this exemption. You are choosing. Name what you're choosing.

## The Unified Insight

Every discipline points to the same conclusion: **there is no view from nowhere.**

You are positioned. Your position creates what you can see and what you cannot. Your desires filter what reaches you. Your fears shape what you avoid. Your strategy determines what you hide. And the structure you inherited set the boundaries before you arrived.

The Coordinate Card doesn't eliminate these constraints. It names them — so they can be examined instead of obeyed.
